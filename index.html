About

Howard Griffin has been a business professor for over 20 years, with a primary focus on international business. His long-standing interest in the world has been shaped by extensive travel and a deep appreciation for languages and cultures.

Beyond academia, he has been an investor for over 35 years and favors a simple, holistic, and long-term approach to building wealth and making sound financial decisions.

“A meaningful life is built through curiosity, discipline, long-term thinking, and a willingness to keep learning.”

Areas of Focus

A blend of academic experience, long-term investing, global perspective, and personal passion.

International Business

More than two decades in higher education, with a particular emphasis on international business, global thinking, and helping students understand the wider world.

Long-Term Investing

Over 35 years of investing experience shaped by patience, simplicity, and a broad, balanced view of financial life.

Languages & Travel

Extensive travel and a lifelong interest in languages have played a major role in both his professional and personal outlook.

Teaching & Research Interests

Howard’s work sits at the intersection of international business, finance, monetary systems, markets, and global development.

Teaching Areas

International business, global markets, business strategy, and the broader economic forces that shape cross-border decision making.

Research Themes

Central banking, gold and Bitcoin, monetary innovation, financial markets, development issues, and the interaction between institutions and economic outcomes.

Professional Perspective

A long-term, globally informed perspective shaped by decades of teaching, investing, travel, and curiosity about how systems evolve over time.

Collection Style

The collection leans heavily toward classic masculine perfumery, darker resinous material, tobacco and boozy compositions, and a mix of niche originals and thoughtfully chosen clone interpretations. It feels less like a random accumulation and more like a personal fragrance library built around character, history, and mood.

There is a clear affection here for vintage men's fragrances from the 1970s through the early 2000s, especially the kind that project presence, texture, and a strong point of view.
